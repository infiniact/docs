IABar — Privacy Policy

Effective: 27 June 2026 · INFINIACT CO., LTD

Short version. IABar is a browser extension that runs an AI agent locally. You bring your own LLM provider API key (BYOK). Your key, your conversations, and any page content you attach are sent directly from your browser to the LLM provider you choose — never to an INFINIACT server. IABar has no backend and no account. We do not sell your data, and we do not run analytics or tracking by default.

1. Who we are

IABar (“the extension”) is published by INFINIACT CO., LTD (“INFINIACT”, “we”). This policy explains what the extension does with your data. It does not cover the third-party LLM providers you choose to connect (see §5).

2. Our model: bring-your-own-key, local-first

IABar does not have its own AI service or server. It calls the API of the LLM provider you configure (e.g. Anthropic, DeepSeek), using your API key. All requests go directly from your browser to that provider. INFINIACT operates no proxy, relay, or backend that your data passes through.

3. What data the extension handles, and where it goes

DataWhere it’s storedWhere it’s sent
Your provider API key Locally, in the browser’s chrome.storage.local, on your device. Only to your chosen provider’s API, to authenticate your requests. Never to INFINIACT.
Chat messages you type Locally, as conversation history in chrome.storage.local. To your chosen provider, to generate a response.
Page content you attach with @ Held in memory for the request; not persisted by us beyond the conversation history you keep locally. To your chosen provider, only as context for the message you send. Read only when you reference a page and grant that site’s permission (see §4).

IABar has no user account, collects no name, email, or identifier, and sends nothing to INFINIACT’s servers (we don’t operate any for this purpose).

4. Page access is on demand, not always-on

IABar does not install a content script on your pages and does not request broad “read all your data on all websites” access up front. It reads a page only when you explicitly reference it with @ in the chat. At that moment the browser asks you to grant access to that specific site; only after you allow it does IABar read that page (its title, URL, your selection, and visible text) to use as context. You can revoke a site’s access at any time in your browser’s extension settings.

5. Third-party LLM providers

When you send a message, the relevant data (your messages, attached page content, and your key for authentication) is transmitted to the LLM provider you selected. That provider’s handling of your data is governed by their privacy policy and terms, not ours:

  • Anthropic — anthropic.com/legal/privacy
  • DeepSeek — platform.deepseek.com (see their privacy terms)

Choose a provider whose data practices you are comfortable with.

7. Storage and retention

Settings and conversations are stored locally on your device via the browser’s extension storage. They stay until you delete a conversation in IABar, clear the extension’s data, or remove the extension. We have no copy of them.

8. Analytics and tracking

IABar does not include third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking, and is off by default for any optional diagnostics. We do not build user profiles and do not sell or share data with data brokers.

9. Children

IABar is a general productivity tool and is not directed to children under 13.

10. Changes

We may update this policy as the extension evolves. Material changes will be reflected here with a new effective date.
